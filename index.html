Gifford Wright Brooks

Adventurer, Learner, Cruciverbalist





My friends call me Giff.

These days, I live in San Francisco and work in fintech.
How did I get here?

  • I grew up in Baltimore and attended Gilman School through graduation. In high school, I played soccer and geeked out on the quiz bowl team. I've since lived in Philadelphia, London, DC, San Francisco, and soon Chicago, but I always consider Baltimore home.

  • After Haverford, I joined to Cornerstone Research, an economic consulting firm. During my four years there, I led teams performing complex analyses for fascinating, high-stakes litigation cases. My projects involved banks, private equity firms, foodservice distributors, animation companies, and electronics manufacturers.
  • I moved to suburban Philadelphia to attend Haverford College, a tight-knit community of thinkers and doers. There, I studied Mathematical Economics, tutored other students, and continued playing soccer. For my junior year, I studied abroad at the LSE.

  • I joined SoFi in early 2016, applying my financial and analytical skills to their credit team. I now conduct research on the company's loan performance and provide credit policy recommendations to the CEO, CFO, and Chief Risk Officer. I am inspired by the positive impact that SoFi has on its 200,000+ members' lives.




Here's what matters to me.

The Outdoors. I fell in love with the outdoors when I moved to San Francisco several years ago. Northern California, and the entire American West, has remarkable natural resources. In the past few years, I have hiked Half Dome in Yosemite; watched the sunrise at the Grand Canyon; explored waterfalls in the Columbia River Gorge; wandered through otherwordly crevasses in Antelope Canyon; solo-hiked Hawaii's treacherous Kalalau Trail; and camped in numerous nearby state parks. I love the solitude and serenity that nature offers—a rejuvenating counterweight to the chaos of city life.

Finance for Good. The public image of finance is justifiably tarnished. I still believe that financial institutions can be engines for economic growth and widespread prosperity, by channelling capital from where it is plentiful to where it is scarce. But the system only works if consumers are treated fairly in the process. At SoFi, I am proud to help grow a modern finance company that is invested in its members success.

Crosswords. I started doing crosswords as a kid, when my parents would let me try out the Sunday New York Times puzzle. I have been hooked ever since. In addition to solving them, I occasionally create my own themed puzzles as well. A highlight of my college career occurred when Will Shortz, editor of the New York Times Crossword, visited campus .
